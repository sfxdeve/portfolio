  - title: Trust began before anyone posted a job.
    claim: Clients needed a reason to believe the people they were considering. Artisans needed their experience to be visible without having their contact details exposed.
    decision: Show focused public profiles with verification badges, work photos, completed-job history, category-specific reviews, and Cape Town availability—but no direct contact details or external links.
    reasoning: The profile offered useful proof while keeping the eventual job inside the protected flow. Verification was shown precisely so it did not read as a blanket guarantee of workmanship.

ArtisanConnect shipped as more than a directory. Clients could describe a job, compare informed quotes, fund the work, review completion evidence, and leave feedback tied to a real paid engagement. Artisans kept control of what they charged and gained a record of completed work.

The lasting judgment was that trust needs both reassurance and restraint. The product had to protect people firmly at the moments that mattered while remaining simple enough to feel like help—not supervision.
