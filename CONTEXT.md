# Portfolio

This context defines the portfolio's project-specific language. Use these terms in public copy, issues, PRDs, and implementation notes so the site stays aligned with the design specification.

## Language

**Primary Audience**:
Hiring managers considering the author for senior product engineering roles. Prospective clients and design or engineering peers are secondary audiences who should still find the evidence credible.
_Avoid_: Everyone, visitors, users

**Portfolio**:
The public evidence-led site for a senior product engineer. It should present selected work, judgment, collaboration, and contact fit without becoming a generic project gallery or resume replacement.
_Avoid_: Personal site, agency brochure, project gallery

**Senior Product Engineer**:
The role presented by the portfolio: someone who shapes complex product work and implements it through launch or operation. Use it when the copy needs the primary professional identity.
_Avoid_: Full-stack developer, software engineer, freelancer

**Core Proposition**:
The lead claim that the engineer brings complex digital products from idea to reality. It anchors the homepage and should be supported by concrete work instead of repeated as a slogan.
_Avoid_: Tagline, elevator pitch, brand promise

**Selected Work**:
The intentionally chosen set of projects shown in the portfolio. It is not a claim about the author's total experience or total project count.
_Avoid_: Project list, portfolio items, all work

**EcoBuiltConnect**:
A shipped marketplace for sustainable and surplus construction materials. Treat it as selected shipped work when source material supports the specific claim being made.
_Avoid_: Construction app, marketplace project, EcoBuilt

**ArtisanConnect**:
A shipped marketplace for finding and hiring artisans. Treat it as selected shipped work when source material supports the specific claim being made.
_Avoid_: Artisan marketplace, hiring app, craft platform

**RushUploads**:
A shipped product for temporary large-file delivery. Treat it as selected shipped work when source material supports the specific claim being made.
_Avoid_: File uploader, upload tool, transfer app

**Fraud Detection System**:
An unshipped product and architecture exploration unless later evidence establishes use in a real workflow. Present it as an exploration, not as selected shipped work.
_Avoid_: Secure Swipe, shipped fraud product, banking product

**Shipped Product**:
A product usable by its intended users in a real workflow. A private pilot can qualify, but a demo, plan, simulation, or test does not.
_Avoid_: Live project, completed project, production app

**Exploration**:
An unshipped academic, speculative, or prototype investigation that demonstrates thinking, craft, or range. It must be labeled separately from shipped work.
_Avoid_: Case study, shipped project, product

**Case Study**:
An evidence-led product story that connects context, decisions, contribution, visual proof, and restrained outcomes. It is not an essay, blog post, or feature inventory.
_Avoid_: Article, blog post, write-up

**Claim**:
A concise statement about what mattered, what changed, or what decision was made. Claims should be supported by source material, visual proof, or reasonable synthesis.
_Avoid_: Assertion, selling point, blurb

**Evidence**:
The material that supports a claim, such as screenshots, flows, diagrams, documented decisions, constraints, and public-safe outcomes. Evidence earns conclusions about complexity and judgment.
_Avoid_: Asset, content, proof point

**Visual Proof**:
A screenshot, sequence, annotation, diagram, or comparison placed near the claim it supports. It should help visitors inspect the work rather than decorate the page.
_Avoid_: Mockup, image, visual

**Public-Safe Source**:
Source material approved for portfolio use, including project names, PRDs, screenshots, diagrams, and redacted or anonymized notes. It sets the boundary for what can be published.
_Avoid_: Client material, raw source, private docs

**Reconstructed Material**:
Evidence recreated from confidential work while preserving the real shape of the workflow. Label it where relevant and never use it to invent functionality or results.
_Avoid_: Mock data, placeholder, approximation

**Outcome**:
A supported result, enablement, or change from the work. Do not imply adoption, scale, revenue, performance, or business impact unless the source material supports it.
_Avoid_: Metric, impact, result

**Working Philosophy**:
The homepage section that connects seniority to observable behavior: staying close to the problem, making clear decisions, handling trade-offs, and remaining responsible for the outcome.
_Avoid_: Values, principles, manifesto

**Contact Invitation**:
The closing invitation for hiring managers, technical founders, product leads, or engineering managers to discuss difficult product work. It should filter for meaningful product context without sounding defensive.
_Avoid_: CTA, availability, hire me

**Portfolio Implementation**:
The technology and architecture used to build this portfolio. Keep it separate from the technologies and engineering range demonstrated by the featured work.
_Avoid_: Client stack, project stack, case-study technology
